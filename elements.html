UI Elements for ADHD-Friendly Design

At MindClarity, we strive to create an interface that is not only visually appealing but also optimized for neurodivergent minds. Here are some of the user interface elements we've carefully crafted to support individuals with ADHD.

Typography

We use large, bold fonts with high contrast for improved readability and visual processing. Headings follow a clear hierarchy to aid information chunking and navigation.

Body Text

Our body text is set in a dyslexia-friendly font with optimized spacing and line height. This reduces visual noise and makes reading more comfortable for individuals with ADHD and other neurodivergent conditions.

Emphasis

We use bold and italicized text sparingly, reserving it for important or emphasized content. This prevents information overload and helps focus attention on key points.

Colors

Our color palette is carefully curated to provide a calming yet energizing atmosphere. Blues and greens promote relaxation, while warm accents like orange and purple add vibrant highlights. We maintain high contrast and avoid overly saturated colors to prevent visual overwhelm.

Forms

Our forms are designed to be clear and uncluttered, with large input fields and prominent labels. We use placeholders to provide context without overwhelming the visual space.
